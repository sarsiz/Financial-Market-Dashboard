# Bond Regimes

- Rising front-end yields usually signal tighter policy expectations or stickier inflation.
- Bull steepening often points to easing expectations or recession concern.
- Bear steepening often points to higher term premium, supply, or stronger growth/inflation repricing.
- Flattening helps highlight late-cycle conditions where policy is restrictive and duration is pressured.
- Always separate: policy path, inflation path, growth path, liquidity path, and event-risk path.
